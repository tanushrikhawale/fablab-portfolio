Laser Cutting

This 7-day module introduced me to laser cutting — a subtractive manufacturing process that uses a high-powered laser to cut, engrave, and shape materials. From understanding the basics to creating my final cardboard bridge project, this journey gave me deep insight into design-to-fabrication workflows.

Day 1 – Introduction & Applications

I explored what laser cutting is and how it’s used in real-world applications such as prototyping, architecture, signage, and art. I also learned about different types of lasers: CO2 (best for wood/acrylic), Fiber (metals), and Diode (compact, lightweight). Safety was emphasized—always wear protective glasses and never leave the machine unattended. ✅ Activity: I listed 5 common laser-cut items including jewelry, phone stands, stencils, keychains, and lamp shades.

Day 2 – Hardware & Software

Learned the main parts of a laser cutter: laser tube, focusing lens, mirrors, bed, air assist, exhaust. Software used: LaserCad. I understood the difference between Vector (cut paths) and Raster (engraving/filling). ✅ Activity: Designed a basic shape (circle + square) in vector mode and exported as .dxf.

Day 3 – Design Basics

Learned to differentiate cut vs engrave settings. I also studied tolerances for press-fit joints (0.2–0.4mm gaps). ✅ Activity: Designed a small box with engraved logo and prepared the file for cutting.

Day 4 – Materials & Settings

Tested different materials: wood, acrylic (transparent/colored), cardboard, and paper. Learned how speed, power, and frequency must be balanced:

  • High power + low speed = deep cuts, risk of burns
  • Low power + high speed = engraving
  • Medium balance = clean edges
✅ Activity: Ran a speed/power test grid on cardboard to find the best settings.

Day 5 – Operating the Laser

Learned how to focus the lens using the calibration tool, set the bed height, enable air assist and exhaust for safety. The process of sending files involved connecting via USB and selecting parameters in LaserCad. ✅ Activity: Cut my first real design successfully while performing all safety checks (like locating the emergency stop).

Day 6 – Project Day

The project was to create a cardboard bridge. I prepared the design files with multiple interlocking parts, nested them efficiently to save material, and then sent the design to the laser cutter. ✅ Activity: Completed the cutting and assembled the bridge structure.

Day 7 – Review & Maintenance

Learned common troubleshooting steps (handling burn marks, incomplete cuts, misalignment). Practiced cleaning mirrors and lens to maintain machine performance. ✅ Activity: Presented my cardboard bridge project and reflected on the improvements I could make, such as refining tolerances for stronger joints.

Final Project – Cardboard Bridge

This was the culmination of the module. The bridge required careful design, precise parameter testing, and assembly skills. The result was a sturdy cardboard structure.

✅ Learning Outcomes

  • Mastered the design-to-fabrication workflow of laser cutting.
  • Understood speed, power, and material settings for clean results.
  • Successfully fabricated a functional cardboard bridge as a team project.
