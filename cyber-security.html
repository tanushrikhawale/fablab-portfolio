Networking

This 7-day module gave me an in-depth understanding of computer networking — how devices communicate, how networks are structured, and how protocols ensure data reaches safely. I also touched on cyber security practices to protect these networks from threats.

Day 1 – Networking Basics

I began by learning what a network is — a system where devices (computers, smartphones, IoT gadgets) connect to share data and resources. Key components include sender, receiver, transmission medium, protocol, and message.

Different network types were introduced:

  • LAN – Local networks within a building.
  • MAN – City-wide networks.
  • WAN – Large networks like the Internet.

Activity: Drew a home network showing how my laptop and phone connect through a WiFi router.

Day 2 – OSI & TCP/IP Models

I explored the OSI 7-layer model and the TCP/IP 4-layer model. Each layer has responsibilities — e.g., the Network Layer handles addressing and routing, while the Transport Layer ensures reliable data delivery using TCP/UDP.

Encapsulation was a big takeaway: data is wrapped in headers as it travels down the OSI stack and unwrapped at the receiver. 

ping 192.168.1.2

Activity: Used Packet Tracer to connect PCs and verified with ping.

Day 3 – IP Addressing & Subnetting

Learned IPv4 addressing, classes (A, B, C), and private vs public IPs. Subnetting was initially tricky but made sense after dividing 192.168.1.0/24 into smaller networks.

Example configuration: 

PC1: 192.168.1.10  Subnet: 255.255.255.0  Gateway: 192.168.1.1
PC2: 192.168.1.20  Subnet: 255.255.255.0  Gateway: 192.168.1.1

Activity: Configured IPs manually on two PCs and tested connectivity.

Day 4 – Routing & Switching

The session focused on devices: Switches (work at Layer 2, MAC-based) and Routers (Layer 3, IP-based). I configured static routes in Packet Tracer to enable communication across LANs. 

Router> enable
Router# configure terminal
Router(config)# ip route 192.168.2.0 255.255.255.0 10.0.0.2

Activity: Linked two LANs and enabled cross-LAN communication.

Day 5 – Cyber Security Fundamentals

Learned about the CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability). Explored types of attacks like Phishing, DDoS, and SQL Injection. Understood the role of firewalls and antivirus software in prevention.

Activity: Identified phishing email patterns and discussed prevention strategies.

Day 6 – Hands-on Security

Hands-on session with Kali Linux. Used tools like Nmap to scan open ports and Wireshark to capture live packets. This showed why encrypted communication (HTTPS) is critical. 

# Scan open ports
nmap 192.168.1.1

# Capture live traffic
wireshark

Activity: Captured HTTP packets and saw usernames/passwords in plaintext.

Day 7 – Secure Networking Practices

Final day focused on defensive measures. Implemented WPA2 security on WiFi, SSL/TLS certificates, and VPN basics. Discussed real-world breaches like WannaCry and how companies mitigate them.

Activity: Configured WPA2 on Packet Tracer routers and enabled firewall rules.

✅ Learning Outcomes

  • Mastered OSI model, IP addressing, and subnetting.
  • Configured routers & switches in Packet Tracer.
  • Understood common cyber threats and countermeasures.
  • Performed basic penetration testing with Kali Linux tools.
  • Learned secure networking best practices.
